<h1>
Faculty </h1>
</div>
<p>BCB faculty are divided into two categories: <strong>Core</strong> and <strong>Resource</strong>. Faculty designated as <strong>Core</strong> have all the components required to be sole mentors for BCB students, namely they:</p>
<ol>
<li>Drive their own research program;</li>
<li>Have sufficient expertise in computational approaches to provide computational-oriented mentoring;</li>
<li>Have sufficient expertise in biological applications to provide biological applications mentoring.</li>
</ol>
<p>BCB faculty designated as <strong>Resource</strong> fulfill some but not all of the above criteria. For example, they may be experts in a computational or biological domain but are not trained in both. Students who choose <strong>Resource</strong> faculty as mentors are required to have co-mentors to ensure the above criteria are collectively fulfilled. For example, a <strong>Resource </strong>faculty who is a principal investigator doing mostly wet bench research could co-advise a BCB student with a mentor in computational research (who could themselves could be <strong>Core</strong> or <strong>Resource</strong>).</p>
<p>If you are a faculty member who would like to join BCB, go to <a href="https://bcb.unc.edu/joining-the-bcb-faculty/">Joining the BCB Faculty</a>.</p>
